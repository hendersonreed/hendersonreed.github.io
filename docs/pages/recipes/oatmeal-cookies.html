My mom’s oatmeal cookies

These are the cookies my mom always made us growing up. They’re mostly oats, and require very few ingredients. They are somewhat “healthy”, in a granola kind of way.

This recipe makes around 9 medium cookies.

ingredients:

  • 150g oats
  • 4g salt
  • 65g sugar
  • 115g self-raising flour (can be substituted, as this is really just pre-mixed flour and baking powder.)
  • 50g fat of some sort (melted butter works, vegetable oil, even olive oil would probably be fine.)
  • 120g water (or milk, oat milk, almond milk, etc.)
  • chocolate chips as desired.

optional add-ins

  • walnuts
  • sunflower seeds
  • dried cranberries
  • almonds

process:

  1. begin heating your oven to 375F.
  2. mix your dry ingredients (not including chocolate chips)
  3. add your fat, and work it into the dry ingredients. It should form a sort of sandy, pebbly mixture. Use your hands to ensure there are no large clumps.
  4. add your wet ingredients.
  5. add your chocolate chips (around 150g is a good amount.)
  6. portion onto a parchment-lined baking sheet into 9 equal scoops.
  7. bake for 18-22 minutes, removing once the craggy bits are starting to get golden.
